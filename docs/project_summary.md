# Project Summary

## Background

Eswatini has the highest adult HIV prevalence in the world. Adolescent girls and young women (AGYW) experience a disproportionate burden of HIV infection.

## Objective

To evaluate associations between educational attainment, early sexual debut, and HIV status among AGYW aged 15–24 years using nationally representative survey data collected in 2016 and 2021.

## Methods

- SHIMS2 (2016) and SHIMS3 (2021)
- Nationally representative survey design
- Survey weights and jackknife replicate weights
- Logistic regression models

## Key Findings

- HIV prevalence declined between survey rounds.
- Educational attainment remained strongly protective.
- Early sexual debut was not independently associated with HIV after adjustment.

## Skills Demonstrated

- SAS programming
- Survey-weighted analysis
- Logistic regression
- Data management
- Public health interpretation
